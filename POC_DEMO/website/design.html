Design Principles

Sound over sight

Visual overlays don't work when you can't see the periphery.

Spatial audio with bearing (left/right/front) alerts users to lateral hazards in real-time. RP users depend on auditory cues and have limited value for visual overlays ‚Äî making audio the primary output modality.
üî≤ Minimal UI

3 buttons only

Start, Pause, End. No complex menus or tiny controls.

Large touch targets optimized for users with reduced contrast sensitivity and field loss. Reduced clutter prevents mis-taps and cognitive overload during navigation. All controls within thumb reach for one-handed use.
‚ö° Concise Warnings

2-5 words max

"Vehicle left, 3 meters." Instant, actionable alerts.

Verbose descriptions create cognitive overload while walking. High recall for lateral hazards takes priority over detailed scene descriptions. Users need to react quickly ‚Äî not process paragraphs.
